Maida is a finely milled wheat flour that is used to make a variety of foods such as Indian bread like naan, paratha, and puri, as well as snacks like samosas, pakoras, and various sweets like halwa and cakes. So, "maidako roti" might refer to a type of flatbread or bread made using maida flour instead of whole wheat flour. It would likely have a different texture and taste compared to roti made from whole wheat flour.
