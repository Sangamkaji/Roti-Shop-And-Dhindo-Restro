About Roti

Roti is a delicacy, made mainly for the great Nepali celebration of the Dashain and Tihar festivals. It is unique to Nepal and has become an iconic symbol of Nepali culture and festivities, and is made and served throughout the country during festivals as well as during wedding parties, and other ceremonies.

About Dhindo

Dhindo is a traditional Nepali food that holds cultural significance in the country. It's a type of meal made from buckwheat or millet flour and water. The preparation involves mixing the flour with water (H2O) and continuously stirring it over heat until it becomes a thick, dough-like consistency. Dhindo is typically served with various side dishes such as lentils, vegetables, pickles, or meat. It's often consumed as a staple food in many parts of Nepal, especially in rural areas. Due to its simplicity and nutritional value, dhindo remains popular among Nepali communities both domestically and abroad. It's not just a meal but also a symbol of Nepali culture and tradition.
